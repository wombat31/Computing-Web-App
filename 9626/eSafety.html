E-safety and health and safety

I.T. is everywhere, in every job nowadays. It is important to ensure that users of Information Technology remain safe in the virtual and physical worlds.

This resource is not exhaustive.

Please be patient whilst this resource is built, one topic at a time.

Syllabus Requirements

Candidates should be able to

    explain why personal data should be kept confidential
    describe how personal data can be gathered by unauthorised persons (including: by smishing vishing, phishing and pharming), and how this might be prevented
    discuss why e-safety is necessary
    describe malware issues (including: Trojan Horse, worms, spyware, adware, rootkit, malicious bots, ransomware)
    describe a range of potential health issues that could arise from using IT
    describe a range of safety issues relating to the use of IT.

E-safety

Personal data on the internet should not be freely available. Bank details, medical records, financial circumstances and other sensitive data, such as political opinions should be kept private. Revealing personal can present the opportunity for people to exploit your personal situation. Safeguards and security has to be in place to prevent this from happening. It is false to assume that you are safe online.
